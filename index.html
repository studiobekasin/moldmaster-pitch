The Rapid Prototyping Bottleneck

Small businesses and amateurs face massive hurdles when moving from a digital 3D model to a physical mold. The industry tools are gatekept by complexity and strict file requirements.

Non-Manifold Mesh Failures

Most software outright rejects standard 3D meshes downloaded from the internet. Amateurs do not know how to fix non-manifold geometry, halting production before it starts.

High Cost of Iteration

Hiring engineers to convert messy files into proper CAD formats for tooling costs thousands of dollars and weeks of time, killing startup momentum.

Fragmented Tooling

Different software is needed for open tray molds versus two-part injection molds. Users are forced to learn multiple complex environments.
